# Attribution

luckiest-thinker is a derivative work of the "refract" Agent Skill by Anthropic
(anthropic-skills collection), used under its MIT license. The core protocol
(mode noticing, stratified enumeration, frequency-calibrated weighting,
secondary-mode capping) originates there.

Research the protocol is built on:
- Zhang et al. (2025), "Verbalized Sampling: How to Mitigate Mode Collapse and
  Unlock LLM Diversity", arXiv:2510.01171
- Misaki & Akiba (2026), "String Seed of Thought", ICLR 2026, arXiv:2510.21150
- Hu et al. (2023), "Amortizing Intractable Inference in Large Language Models",
  ICLR 2024 (GFlowNet proportional sampling principle)

Luckiest-edition changes (effort tiers, hybrid RNG selection, self-check gate,
session anti-repetition) are (c) luckiest, MIT licensed.
